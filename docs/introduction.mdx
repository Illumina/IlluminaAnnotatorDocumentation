Nirvana provides clinical-grade annotation of genomic variants (SNVs, MNVs, insertions, deletions, indels, STRs, gene fusions, and SVs (including CNVs). It can be run as a stand-alone package or integrated into larger software tools that require variant annotation.

The input to Nirvana are VCFs and the output is a [structured JSON representation](file-formats/nirvana-json-file-format.mdx) of all annotation and sample information (as extracted from the VCF). Nirvana handles multiple alternate alleles and multiple samples with ease.

The software is being developed under a rigorous SDLC and testing process to ensure accuracy of the results and enable embedding in other software with regulatory needs. Nirvana uses a continuous integration pipeline where millions of variant annotations are monitored against baseline values daily.

Backronym: **NI**mble and **R**obust **VA**riant a**N**not**A**tor

## What does Nirvana annotate?

### Variant positions

 - chromosome & position
 - ref & alt alleles
 - quality & filters
 - cytogenetic bands

### Samples

 - genotypes
 - variant frequencies
 - allele depths
 - copy numbers
 - *de novo* variant flags

### Alternate alleles

 - variant identifiers
 - chromosome & positions
 - ref & alt alleles
 - HGVS g. nomenclature
 - PhyloP scores
 - regulatory regions
 - recomposed variants (converting nearby SNVs that share haplotypes into MNVs)
 - external data sources (e.g. dbSNP, gnomAD, ClinVar)

### Transcripts

 - transcript & gene IDs
 - bio type
 - exon & intron overlap
 - cDNA, CDS, and amino acid positions
 - codons & amino acid representation
 - Sequence Ontology consequences
 - HGVS c. and p. nomenclature
 - gene fusions
 - SIFT & PolyPhen

### Genes

 - gene symbol & HGNC ID
 - gene summary
 - external data sources (e.g. OMIM, gnomAD, ClinGen)
